Water Main Breaks

Predicting water main breaks

With an aging water system and hundreds of water main breaks per year, replacing pipes is essential. Given that water main replacement is expensive, and not all pipes need to be replaced at once, creating a system to assess the risk of water mains breaking was critical for efficiency and effectiveness. Workjing with the Data Science for Social Good program from the University of Chicago, we built a predictive model that performs six times better than randomly selecting water mains for replacement. The results of the model have allowed the department to partner with other developers and infrastructure departments to dig once and replace all the underground infrastructure at the same time - saving money and improving service.

Fire Hydrants

Predicting the water flow rate for each fire hydrant

In partnership with the fire department we built a model to predict how much water would flow from each fire hydrant in the city. This information is used to help first responders assess the best fire hydrant to use when putting out a fire.
